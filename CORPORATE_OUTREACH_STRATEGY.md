## Phased Outreach Plan

### PHASE 1: INITIAL CONTACT (Week 1)

**Step 1: Email Tim Welch (VP Biz Dev)**
- Send Template 1: "Strategic Partnership Opportunity"
- Include brief company background
- Request 20-minute call
- Include LinkedIn profile link

**Step 2: Email Dan Figurski (President Employers)**
- Send Template 2: Emphasize employer/champion programs angle
- Reference his specific focus
- Request meeting

**Timing:** Send both Tuesday morning (Pacific time)

**Expected Outcome:** 1-2 responses within 5 days

---

### PHASE 2: FOLLOW-UP (Week 2)

**If No Response from Tim Welch:**
- Send Template 4 (follow-up email)
- Reference original email
- Suggest specific times

**If No Response from Dan Figurski:**
- Send follow-up with different angle
- Highlight employer benefits

**If Response Received:**
- Schedule call immediately
- Send calendar invite with brief agenda
- Prepare presentation materials

**Timing:** Day 7-10 after initial email

---

### PHASE 3: STRATEGIC CALL (Week 2-3)

**Call Objective:**
- Explain Nzone value proposition
- Understand KinderCare's growth priorities
- Identify barriers/concerns
- Get introduction to next stakeholder (if needed)

**Call Agenda (20 min):**
1. Introduction (2 min)
2. Nzone overview (5 min)
3. Partnership opportunity (5 min)
4. Questions (5 min)
5. Next steps (3 min)

**Preparation:**
- Research contact's background/LinkedIn
- Know KinderCare's recent initiatives
- Prepare one-page summary
- Have follow-up materials ready

**Success Outcome:**
- Positive interest
- Introduction to additional stakeholders
- Follow-up meeting scheduled

---

### PHASE 4: ESCALATION (Week 3-4)

**If Tim/Dan Shows Interest:**
- Schedule meeting with COO (Lindsay Sorhondo)
- Provide formal partnership proposal
- Discuss commercial terms
- Get legal review needed

**If No Initial Response:**
- Escalate to COO directly
- Reference industry opportunity
- Emphasize strategic fit

**Timing:** Based on Phase 3 outcome

---

### PHASE 5: FORMAL PROPOSAL (Week 4-6)

**Deliverables:**
- Partnership agreement outline
- Financial terms (revenue share/fees)
- Implementation timeline
- Center rollout plan
- Marketing/communication plan

**Next Steps:**
- Legal review
- Board presentation (if needed)
- Pilot program agreement
- Contract finalization

## Handling Objections

### "We have other programs already"
**Response:** "Understood. Nzone is complementary - you add value to families without adding work. It's a partner, not a replacement."

### "How much does this cost KinderCare?"
**Response:** "Nothing upfront. We handle recruitment, coaching, and operations. We could structure a revenue share if you want to benefit financially."

### "We'd need to pilot it first"
**Response:** "Perfect - that's what we'd propose. Select 10-20 centers for a pilot, measure results, then scale."

### "I need to talk to [other person]"
**Response:** "Great idea. Can you introduce us? Or I can follow up with them directly."

### "Can you send more information?"
**Response:** "Absolutely. I'll send a one-pager and look forward to a quick call to discuss."

## Red Flags & Contingencies

**Red Flag:** No response after two attempts
- **Action:** Wait 2 weeks, try phone call to main number
- **Alternative:** Email CEO assistant directly

**Red Flag:** Contact says "not interested"
- **Action:** Ask for referral to right person
- **Alternative:** Try different angle with different stakeholder

**Red Flag:** Request for detailed proposal but no timeline
- **Action:** Set deadline for feedback
- **Follow-up:** Escalate to COO for priority

**Red Flag:** Lengthy legal/contract process
- **Action:** Accelerate with pilot program approach
- **Alternative:** Propose simplified first agreement
