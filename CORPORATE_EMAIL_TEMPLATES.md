## Corporate Outreach Best Practices

### Timing & Method:
- **Email first** - Executive assistants screen calls
- Send **Tuesday-Thursday**, **9am-11am Pacific**
- Start with **VP Business Development** (Tim Welch) as entry point
- Follow up through **CEO/COO** if needed
- Allow **7-10 days** between touchpoints

### Structure:
1. **Subject line** - Clear benefit/opportunity focus
2. **Opening** - Brief context about your company
3. **Opportunity** - Why partnership makes sense
4. **Value prop** - What KinderCare gains
5. **CTA** - Specific ask (20-minute call)

### Tone:
- Professional but conversational
- Executive-level (not overly casual)
- Focused on business value
- Respectful of time constraints
- Data/results-driven

### Email Addresses:
Since personal emails aren't fully public, use:
- Corporate main: media@kindercare.com
- LinkedIn direct message
- Corporate phone: (503) 872-1300 (ask for person)

## Phone Call Script - Corporate Version

**Opening:**
"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] with Nzone Sports. I sent an email about a potential partnership opportunity with KinderCare. Do you have 60 seconds to chat?"

**If Yes:**
"Great! Nzone operates youth sports leagues in 50+ cities. We partner with childcare providers to bring sports programming to families. Given KinderCare's market position, I think there's a real opportunity here. Would you be open to a brief call next week to explore further?"

**If Hesitant:**
"I understand you're busy. This would just be a 20-minute exploratory call with no obligation. Might you have 30 minutes next Tuesday or Wednesday?"

**If No:**
"No problem. Who might be the right person to discuss partnership opportunities with? [Get referral] Thanks for the referral - I appreciate it."

**Key Points:**
- Professional but confident
- Clear value proposition
- Specific time request
- Easy "no" (lowers resistance)
- Get referral if not the right person
